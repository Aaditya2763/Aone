Understanding PTE

What is PTE?

PTE stands for Pearson Test of English. It is an English proficiency test recognized by over 3,300 universities in the world including prestigious and high ranking Universities like Howard, Oxford , Colombia and Yale University. Universities in most countries with English as Native language like USA, Canada, Australia, UK and New Zealand accept the test. It is primarily used for immigration and studying abroad.

  • Test Format: PTE is a computer-based test that assesses listening, speaking, writing, and reading skills within a single two-hour examination session.
  • Immigration Authorities: PTE is also accepted by several immigration authorities for visa applications.
  • Scoring: The test is scored on a scale from 10 to 90, with 10 being the lowest and 90 the highest.
  • Quick Results: Test results are typically available within the same day to up to 5 business days.

If you’re aiming for success in your PTE test, our classes will provide the necessary tools and strategies to help you achieve your goals!

 

Speaking:

Format:

  • Personal Introduction: Briefly introduce yourself.
  • Read Aloud: Read a short text aloud – (6-7 questions)
  • Repeat Sentence: Repeat a spoken sentence. (10-12 questions)
  • Describe Image: Describe an image (graph, chart, or diagram) within 40 seconds. (2-3 questions)
  • Re-tell Lecture: Summarize a spoken lecture within 40 seconds. (1-2 questions)
  • Answer Short Questions: Provide concise answers to objective questions. (5-6 questions)

Skills Assessed:

  • Oral Fluency: Ability to speak clearly and fluently.
  • Pronunciation: Correct pronunciation and intonation.
  • Content: Coherent and relevant responses.

 

Writing:

Format:

  • Summarize Written Text: Summarize a passage in one sentence. (1-2 questions)
  • Essay: Write an essay (200-300 words) on a given topic. (1-2 questions)

Skills Assessed:

  • Grammar and Vocabulary: Use appropriate language.
  • Content Development: Organize ideas logically.
  • Sentence Structure: Write clear and coherent sentences.

 

Reading:

Format:

  • Multiple-Choice, Choose Single Answer: Select the correct answer. (1-2 questions)
  • Multiple-Choice, Choose Multiple Answers: Select all correct answers. (1-2 questions)
  • Re-order Paragraphs: Arrange jumbled paragraphs. (2-3 questions)
  • Reading Fill in the Blanks: Fill in missing words. (5-6 questions)
  • Reading & Writing Fill in the Blanks: Fill in missing words in a text. (4-5 questions)

Skills Assessed:

  • Comprehension: Understand main ideas and details.
  • Inference: Deduce information from the text.
  • Vocabulary: Identify word meanings in context.

 

Listening:

Format:

  • Summarize Spoken Text: Summarize an audio recording. (1-2 questions)
  • Multiple-Choice, Choose Multiple Answers: Select all correct answers. (1-2 questions)
  • Fill in the Blanks: Fill in missing words. (2-3 questions)
  • Highlight Correct Summary: Identify the correct summary. (2-3 questions)
  • Multiple-Choice, Choose Single Answer: Select the correct answer. (1-2 questions)
  • Select Missing Word: Choose the missing word. (1-2 questions)
  • Highlight Incorrect Words: Identify incorrect words. (2-3 questions)

Skills Assessed:

  • Listening Comprehension: Understand spoken information.
  • Note-Taking: Extract key points.
  • Listening for Specific Information: Identify details.
